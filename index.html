Main Characters Overview

Prospero

The exiled Duke of Milan who controls the island using magical powers. He seeks justice and reconciliation.

Ariel

A spirit bound to serve Prospero, longing for freedom. Represents obedience and unseen influence.

Caliban

Native to the island. Resents Prospero's control. Symbolizes colonization, resistance, and the natural world.

Ferdinand

Son of King Alonso. Falls in love with Miranda. Represents innocence and renewal.

Miranda

Prospero's daughter, raised on the island. Her love with Ferdinand symbolizes hope and peace.

Introduction

Welcome! This exhibition compares Prospero’s magical authority in Shakespeare’s The Tempest to modern-day forms of power—think media moguls, tech billionaires, and political leaders.

Character Profiles

1. Prospero vs. Tech Billionaire

  • Prospero: Controls spirits & environment using his magic staff.
  • Modern Parallel: Tech CEOs shaping global communication & behavior through platforms.

2. Ariel vs. Political Leader

  • Ariel: An invisible agent carries out Prospero’s will.
  • Modern Parallel: Political operatives or media advisors who steer public opinion behind the scenes.

3. Caliban vs. Influential Activist

  • Caliban: Native islander marginalized and punished for resisting Prospero.
  • Modern Parallel: Grassroots activists challenging powerful institutions but often silenced or ignored.
Act & Play Summaries

Act IV, Scene 1

Prospero rewards Ferdinand and Miranda’s love with a masque, halts it to punish conspirators, and demonstrates his total control over his enemies.

Act V, Scene 1

Prospero forgives his foes, renounces magic, restores order, and reveals the lovers—ending with reconciliation and departure home.

Full Play Overview

Prospero uses magic to test and ultimately forgive those who wronged him, arranging love, punishing plots, and seeking redemption before abandoning his powers.

Why Does Power Still Matter Today?

Power still matters today because it shapes how societies function and evolve. From ancient spells to modern algorithms, it influences decisions and behavior. Power determines who has access to resources, opportunity, and representation. It can heal and uplift, or control and marginalize—depending on how it’s used. Understanding its dynamics helps us identify injustice and promote fairness. By recognizing who holds power and why, we become more empowered ourselves.
