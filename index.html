Introduction

Welcome! This exhibition compares Prospero’s magical authority in Shakespeare’s The Tempest to modern-day forms of power—think media moguls, tech billionaires, and political leaders. Scroll through to explore character comparisons, scene summaries, and the enduring relevance of power.

Character Profiles

1. Prospero vs. Tech Billionaire

  • Prospero: Controls spirits & environment using his magic staff.
  • Modern Parallel: Tech CEOs shaping global communication & behavior through platforms.

2. Ariel vs. Political Leader

  • Ariel: An invisible agent carries out Prospero’s will.
  • Modern Parallel: Political operatives or media advisors who steer public opinion behind the scenes.

3. Caliban vs. Influential Activist

  • Caliban: Native islander marginalized and punished for resisting Prospero.
  • Modern Parallel: Grassroots activists challenging powerful institutions but often silenced or ignored.
Act & Play Summaries

Act IV, Scene 1

Prospero rewards Ferdinand and Miranda’s love with a masque, halts it to punish conspirators, and demonstrates his total control over his enemies.

Act V, Scene 1

Prospero forgives his foes, renounces magic, restores order, and reveals the lovers—ending with reconciliation and departure home.

Full Play Overview

The Tempest opens with a storm conjured by Prospero. Exiled Duke Prospero uses magic to test and ultimately forgive those who wronged him, arranging love, punishing plots, and seeking redemption before abandoning his powers.

Why Does Power Still Matter Today?

Power shapes societies, whether through magical spells or algorithms and policymaking. Understanding its dynamics helps us recognize both its potential for healing and its capacity for abuse.
